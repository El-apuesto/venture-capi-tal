What We Do - In Depth

We provide comprehensive grant services:

  • Find grants: We monitor funding opportunities from federal, state, and foundation sources
  • Plan projects: Help towns figure out what they need and how to present it
  • Write applications: Prepare complete, competitive grant proposals
  • Post-award support: Sometimes help with reporting and compliance after they win

Think of us as the grant department these small communities can't afford to hire full-time.

Ã—

Who We Serve - Examples

Our typical clients include:

  • Small towns: Population 500-5,000, maybe one part-time clerk
  • Rural water districts: Serving scattered communities, need infrastructure upgrades
  • Volunteer fire departments: Need equipment but have no grant writer
  • Rural nonprofits: Food banks, senior centers, community clinics

These communities have real needs but limited resources. That's our niche.

Your Work - Detailed Breakdown

Depending on your skills and the project, you might:

  • Research: Search grant databases, read RFPs, identify good matches for clients
  • Write/Edit: Draft sections of applications, edit for clarity, ensure all requirements are met
  • Organize: Track deadlines, create checklists, manage document versions
  • Gather info: Email clients for data, photos, letters of support
  • Format: Prepare final documents according to funder requirements
  • Report: Help write simple progress reports for active grants

Most tasks are well-defined with clear instructions and examples.

Expectations - What Success Looks Like

  • Online work: You'll work remotely using email, cloud documents, and video calls
  • Checklists: We use clear task lists so you always know what's next
  • Deadlines matter: Grant deadlines are hard - we need reliability
  • Ask early: If something's confusing, ask right away - don't guess
  • Quality over speed: Better to do it right than do it fast
  • Honesty: Tell us if you're stuck, behind, or need help

We're looking for someone dependable who communicates well.

Requirements & Workload Details

Workload varies:

  • Grant cycles create busy periods (often in spring and fall)
  • Some weeks might be 5-10 hours, others 20+
  • Occasional urgent deadlines (we try to give notice!)

You'll need:

  • Reliable internet connection
  • Computer with word processing and spreadsheet software
  • Ability to read and follow detailed written instructions
  • Email and basic file management skills

Helpful but not required:

  • Previous grant writing experience
  • Familiarity with rural community needs
  • Project management tools experience
